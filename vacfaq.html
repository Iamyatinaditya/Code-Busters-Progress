Vaccination FAQs



About the Vaccine

COVID-19 affects people of all ages, although morbidity and death are many times greater in adults, especially those over the age of 50. Asymptomatic or mild infection affects children. The standard procedure is to analyse any new vaccination in an older population first, then reduce the age group to assess the vaccine's safety and effectiveness in children. So yet, none of the currently available vaccinations have been tested in children. The efficacy and safety of the COVID-19 vaccinations in children are now being tested in clinical studies.

Covishield is made up of Inactivated adenovirus containing Coronavirus segments, Aluminium Hydroxide Gel, L-Histidine, L-Histidine Hydrochloride Monohydrate, Magnesium Chloride Hexahydrate, Polysorbate 80, Ethanol, Sucrose, Sodium Chloride, and Disodium Edetate Dihydrate are all ingredients in Covishield (EDTA). Inactivated Coronavirus, Aluminum Hydroxide Gel, TLR 7/8 Agonist, 2-Phenoxyethanol, and Phosphate Buffered Saline [NKA1] are all ingredients in Covaxin.



What to expect before the vaccination

Both would be effective in avoiding infection as well as a person's sickness progressing to a severe stage. It would have the long-term benefit of avoiding death in the elderly and those with comorbidities.

Who will get the vaccine

Anyone with confirmed or suspected COVID-19 infection may increase the risk of spreading the same to other people present at the vaccination site. For this reason, infected individuals should defer vaccination for 14 days after symptoms resolution.

Yes, it is advisable to receive complete the schedule of COVID-19 vaccine irrespective of previous history of infection with COVID-19. This will surely help in developing a strong immune system response against this disease. Development of immunity or duration of protection after COVID-19 exposure is not created therefore it is highly recommended to receive vaccine even after the exposure of COVID-19 infection. It is recommended to wait for 4-8 weeks after recovery from COVID symptoms before getting the vaccine.



How will we be vaccinated

India has one of the world's largest immunisation programmes, with over 26 million babies and 29 million pregnant women receiving vaccinations. To efficiently cater to the country's enormous and diversified population, programme mechanisms are being strengthened/geared up.

Before obtaining a licence for a vaccine candidate, our country's drug agency examines the safety and effectiveness data from clinical studies. As a result, any COVID-19 vaccines that are licenced will have similar safety and effectiveness. However, because various COVID-19 vaccines are not interchangeable, it must be guaranteed that just one kind of vaccine is used to complete the whole immunisation regimen.



What to expect after the vaccination?

When a large number of people in a community get vaccinated, herd immunity develops, providing indirect protection. With each illness, the percentage of persons who must be immune in order to acquire herd immunity varies. For example, 95 percent of the population must be vaccinated against measles to begin producing herd immunity, while the proportion of the population that must be immunised against COVID-19 to begin inducing herd immunity is unknown.

Because the vaccinations are not interchangeable, it's critical to get the second dosage of the same vaccine as the first. In addition, the Co-WIN site will aid in ensuring that everyone receives the same vaccine.
